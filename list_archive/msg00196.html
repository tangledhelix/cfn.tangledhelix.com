Hello, 

Upon reflection, I find these resurrected memories from cfn to be quite 
powerful. It is interesting that the experiences that we collectively have 
surrounding cfn have has such an impact. I don't believe that there are 
many things in life that cause such strong memories and emotions. 

I have always felt that one of the most important byproducts/effects of 
removing the need for physical presense in meeting/communicating/creating 
community and friendships is that the relationships formed are not bound 
by place and time, and so they will linger longer than the other fleeting 
experiences of life. 

Anyways, I'm glad to hear from all of you. I am back in Cleveland at the 
present moment in time. I have successfully constructed a virtual 
workplace and currently develop software remotely for people around the 
world. I'm not entirely sure what I will do next, but I am working on 
plans to make my virtual workplace mobile and work over satelite, while 
prehaps moving by bus or if I could swing it, by bike or maybe bike and 
kayak. In my time after cfn, I finished my BS in Computer Science in two 
years, worked some, travelled a lot (38 countries and counting), and got 
married.

-Rolvow
