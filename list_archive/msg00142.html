So, I'm Mike McFarland, always and only Highlandr on IRC. Nowdays I go by Ned, pretty mcuh to differentiate me from all the other Mikes in the world, but I still respond to Mike -- going by a different name seems to confuse people. 

So, some things change:
I just finished writing my doctoral thesis for a Ph.D. in Peace Studies. Yes, I am the king of interdisciplinary liberal arts that have a clear normative aspect. My thesis? Glad you asked: I analysed secular philosophies of religion and how they influence conflict where religion is a factor. Yes, religion and politics -- if I could have included sex, I'd have had the big three Things One Can't Discuss in Polite Society. Well, let's see what my postdoc work brings. And, those of you who remember might recall that I was planning on joining the Army by way of ROTC. And now a degree in peace. Do I win the "how did he get here from there" title? Anyway, I've spent five of the last six years in Bradford, England, pursuing various degrees in that field, and, really, I've been living in a new state or country every year or so since I went off to Pitt in 1994. 

But, more stays the same:
Having finished my doctorate, I've moved back to Cleveland to start a career. So, if anyone local wants to hire a highly academically qualified person with no salable practical skills whatsoever, I'm your man. Seriously, as with any upper-level humanities / social science degree, I'm looking into teaching. In the meantime, due to the no job, I'm even living at my parents'. Fortunately, we get along well. I look pretty much like I used to -- I even bought a new version of the boots and drover coat I used to wear 'cause I liked them so much. And I drive the same white '94 Saturn 4-door as back in the day. Hey, it still runs well and gets nearly 40 MPG highway ... OK, justifications aside, I am still a geek.
I'm glad Dan started this list, 'cause I get nostalgic, and have wondered where everyone got to. As for a picnic, I'm all for it, but if you schedule it when I'm back in England defending the thesis, I will be pissed. That's still unscheduled, but 20-23 July is right out -- I graduate then. Mostly, though I don't thinky ou'll hear from me much here. Even low-traffic lists move to fast for me, mostly 'cause I always post long-ass replies, so I lurk instead.
On finding people, I've got a few contacts lying around my address book, so I'll send them notes. I've also seen that there're a few people that list Cleveland FreeNet as an interest on LiveJournal. Some I recognize, some I don't. I don't want to spam them if someone here actually knows someone and can contact them directly.
Oh, speaking of contacts: Kristen, how did you get my Bradford email address? The archive hasn't updated, so I might have missed something someone posted.
