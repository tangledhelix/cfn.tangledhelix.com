Religious people are ignoring some of the facts. God allowed her to enter this state. 4 decades ago she'd be long dead, no question. Today though we have machines can do everything for you - and keep you alive indefinitely. We need to not just ask the question about the quality of life - but the dignity of life. Mary Schiavo is not an example of dignity. It is an example of selfishness, flying in the face of Nature and God by not letting her go. I understand that sometimes people wake up from comas, but this isn't a coma. They say the CAT scans show there is "nobody home" and yet for some reason her parents and family are determined to keep her alive. I can totally empathize with how they must feel. They are hoping for a miracle. If Christians truly believe in an after life, they have to be come to terms and accept that it's time to let her go. I know it's not easy to face mortality, but I haven't even heard about it as an option in the equation.
Honestly though - we treat our pets with more dignity, as I don't agree with the feeding tube removal. When pets are suffering and in pain in their last days we end their life. When it's a human being we starve them to death? Man interrupted Nature course. It's then our responsibility to then end it with dignity, since we meddled in the first place. This prolonged state only keeps people's morale broken, angry and at odds with one another. Healing cannot happen, it's like an open wound.
Don't get me wrong, I'm not saying that everyone should be performing euthanasia indiscriminately. I believe Nature should always be allowed to take it's course. We have medications that can dull pain and ease suffering in the end-time for people. My point though is in this case we intervened and so we are responsible.
I think sometimes the politicians and religious leaders want it this way -- because they can jab and poke and get a bigger reaction than if she simply was put to sleep and the matter was over with. This is a very private matter that the Congress should not be involved in. They should be worried about health care, corporate corruption, Social Security, etc. The religious people are upset that the "pro-life" agenda politicians are leaving them in the cold, welcome to Washington. Maybe some good will come from this in that people will start to more seriously review political records than just go by someone's re-election promises.
Also Mr. Bush, you cannot be pro-life but be okay about the death penalty. It also disturbs me that "Christians" are holding such animosity towards the husband (whom none know or met) and are prejudging him holding signs saying "Give her life and a divorce!" It's disappointing all around. 

That is all.  :-)
</soapbox>
